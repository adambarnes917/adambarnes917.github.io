What is Lifting?

Lifting is a hobby that I have picked up over the past few years. It started when I was in highschool working out with the basketball team, and has since become a regular part of my own daily routine.

  • Benefits: Going to the gym helps me stay fit and active. It improves my strength and confidence as well.
  • Approaches: I do exercises with freeweights (dumbells) and machines, as well as bodyweight exercises like pull ups.
  • Consistency is the most efficient way to keep steady progress when it comes to lifting weights.

About Me

I've been going to the gym since freshmam/sophomore year of high school, and I believe it has become a bit of my identity. I primarily focus on building muscle, which includes both lifting consistently and eating enough protein to support growth. This hobby has helped me build discipline and a routine that I enjoy.

  • Experience: 5/6 years with varied programs.

My Schedule

The timing of when I workout depends on what I need to get done that day and when I have free time. Also, I have a split that I follow each week. More details on my split are seen in the table below.

  1. Early morning: I don't usually go early in the morning, as I like to sleep in and have class at 10am throughout the week.
  2. Lunch sessions: I usually go around 2:00/3:00 since that time works best for me after class. Also the gym isn't too crowded at this time.
  3. Evening: Sometimes when I lift with my friends I will go later in the day. Sometimes I even lift really late at night.

Where I Lift

I have been to many gyms, but the one that is most convenient for me is the Crunch fitness on University Blvd. It is massive and has all the equipment I could ever need. Other gyms I have been to are listed below.

  • UREC: This gym is very nice and has all the different machines. However, when it gets crowded it makes it tough to finish workouts quickly.
  • YMCA: I've been to a few different YMCA's and they are overall very good. The only downside for the YMCA is the expensive membership price.
  • Anytime Fitness: I love this gym because I can go any time of the day. They are open 24/7. I don't have a membership though since it's far from where I live.

Overall, it doesn't matter where you lift as long as you are consistent and pushing yourself to improve. Any gym with basic equipment will do.

Getting Started

Start small and build habits. Focus on learning movements and having correct form. A big part of going to the gym is staying consistent, so having the discipline to go regularly is key.

  1. Pick a split, whether its Push Pull Legs, Upper Lower, or your own personalized split.
  2. Set a realistic training frequency.
  3. Track progress and adjust.

Purpose & Motivation

The gym has been constant throughout my life. I will always have the ability to work out and push myself there. I use the gym as a way to relieve stress from school and work, and it gives me a sense of accomplishment when I hit new personal records. When I don't feel like going, I remind myself of why I do it:

  • Mental clarity and confidence.
  • Physical resilience and longevity.
  • Community and shared goals.
